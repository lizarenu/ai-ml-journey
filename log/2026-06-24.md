- Worked through L1 vs L2 regularization starting from bias-variance (large weights = high sensitivity = can fit noise = high variance). Derived L2's multiplicative weight decay (`w*(1-2*lr*lambda)`, shrinks proportional to current weight, never hits exactly 0) vs. L1's fixed-magnitude pull (`lr*lambda*sign(w)`, can overshoot small weights past zero, and gets parked at exactly 0 for unimportant weights when the L1 pull dominates a near-zero true gradient).
